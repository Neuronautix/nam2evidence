Context of Use Tab
==================

The Context of Use tab defines the purpose and boundaries of the evidence
package. It should be reviewed before interpreting any study or endpoint data.

Fields shown
------------

The card includes:

* NAM type;
* development stage;
* regulatory confidence level;
* regulatory question;
* intended use;
* decision supported;
* biological domain and endpoint class;
* population relevance;
* documented limitations;
* acceptance criteria.

Editing
-------

1. Click **Edit**.
2. Update the editable text fields.
3. Click **Save**.

Use conservative language. The Context of Use should describe what the evidence
can support, not overclaim what it proves.

How to review
-------------

Ask these questions:

* Is the regulatory question specific?
* Does the intended use match the evidence available?
* Are limitations explicit?
* Are acceptance criteria measurable?
* Is the confidence level justified by the data?

Common problems
---------------

Over-broad context:
  The app may look complete, but the evidence may not support the claimed use.

Missing limitations:
  Missing limitations weaken review transparency.

Unclear decision:
  If the decision supported is vague, downstream claims and exports become hard
  to interpret.
